## The Problem No One Solves

Doctors see the same patient twice.  
The second visit starts from zero.

Healthcare doesn’t have a knowledge problem.  
It has a **continuity problem**.

A patient with Type 2 diabetes visits:

- Dr. A → prescribes Metformin  
- Follow-up scheduled  
- Risk notes added  

Next visit:

- Different doctor  
- Same patient  
- Zero context  

Everything resets.

The patient repeats everything.

---

## Why This Happens

Medical knowledge already exists:

- clinical guidelines  
- drug references  
- treatment protocols  

Systems today are very good at retrieving this.

But they cannot answer:

> What happened to this patient last time?

Because that answer is not in documents.

It lives in **history**.

---

## What We Built

Instead of trying to force retrieval to solve this, we introduced a **memory-first system**.

### xysq Memory Layer (Core System)

- Persistent patient memory via MCP  
- Session-independent  
- Shared across staff  

Stores:

- symptoms  
- diagnosis  
- medications  
- doctor notes  
- follow-ups  

This creates a **continuous patient timeline**.

---

### Where RAG Fits (Secondary)

RAG is still useful, but only for:

- general medical knowledge  
- guidelines  
- reference queries  

It is **not responsible for patient continuity**.
